In: Poppygo

The poppy plant is an herbaceous perennial, often appearing in shades of bright orange to deep scarlet with cup-shaped petals. The papaver orientale (oriental poppy) bloom in mid-summer, and originate from Central Asia. The poppy plant is renowned for its visual beauty and striking color, however, conversely, also for their illicit innards and their hand in the contentious history of 19th century China. The dichotomy of the poppy inspires Poppygo. A colorful corner on your local street; Poppygo boasts delicious Asian snacks and vibrant scenery, while also holding hundreds of unique stories in its shelves, containing everything from sweet romances to sinister mysteries. Find a new favorite story, grab a quick noodle lunch, or both—pick your poison?

Poppygo started as a dream between two friends—a writer and a cook—who wanted to create a space that felt like home. Inspired by the poppy flower, with its bold beauty and complicated history, they imagined a place that could hold both joy and depth. Blending a love of Asian literature with nostalgic snacks, they transformed an old corner shop into a cozy cafe-library. Shelves filled with manga, poetry, and forgotten classics sit beside steaming bowls of noodles and sweet milk tea. It’s a place for contrasts—light and dark, sweet and spicy, romance and mystery.

At Poppygo, every bite tells a story. A flakey taro puff might remind you of a midnight train ride in Taipei; the sesame mochi donut, of a childhood festival you almost forgot. Bowls of spicy instant noodles come dressed up with soft eggs and scallions—fast food made slowly, with care. Sweet, savory, strange, or simple—our menu changes often, but always with a wink to memory and a nod to home. The shelves at Poppygo are a maze of voices—soft love stories, sharp-edged thrillers, dreamy manga, and forgotten folktales.
